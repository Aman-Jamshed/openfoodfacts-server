Update a product from the mobile app

You need the Android Open Food Facts app to update a product that way. You can get it there.

1. Check for any photo or data mismatch

Scan any product using its barcode. Check if the front photo seems to match the current packaging. You can slide up to compare all the values and photos.

If you find any mismatch, it would be great to update the product by providing new photos and new data. If you don't have time to change the data, new photos will be great, since it will empower another contributor to do it without having the product in his or her hands.

2. Open the product to send new photos

Slide up the card to get the full product. Tap on the photo to open the photo edit mode. If this is the first time you open the photo edit mode, you will see a small tutorial.

3. Take a new front photo

Tap on the little camera icon to take a new front photo. Try to avoid any personal details and if you can take the photo on a white background, all the better.

Once you are happy with a photo, validate it.

Open Food Facts will propose to crop the photo. Prefer a wide crop to a narrow crop as this can be adjusted later by you or another contributor. We don’t have to lose any valuable product information.

Once you are happy with the crop, tap on “Validate”.

You should see a little spinning icon at the bottom during the photo upload. You can move to ingredients without waiting for the upload to complete. Once the upload completes, the new photo should appear.

3. Take a new ingredients photo

Do the same process again with the ingredients photo. For ingredients, a precise crop is more important as it will be used to perform automatic text extraction, known as OCR.

Hint: if you have a hard time cropping from the edge of your screen, you can rotate the picture and crop from the top. Once you are satisfied with a crop, you can rotate it back in the right orientation.

If a product has multiple languages for the ingredients list, you can add all those languages using the drop down menu called “Languages”.

Repeat the photo taking process for each language. If no photo for the language is selected, you will see the drop down appear in yellow, as well as a little message below. Make sure that the photo you are taking is in the right language when compared to the dropdown.

Hint: If you are in a rush, you can take a wide photo of all the ingredients in all languages and it will be possible to crop more precisely later in all languages, even if you have thrown away the packaging.

3. Take a new nutrition photo

You can now repeat the process with the nutrition table. Usually nutrition tables are multilingual. So you can limit yourself to the main language of the product. If the nutrition table is in the form of a separate text, you can of course make a different photo crop for each language.

4. Well done !

We have now updated all the photos. Well done ! You can go back to the product by tapping on the back row on the top right of the screen.

5. Updating outdated nutrition values and ingredient lists.

Now we need to make sure that the values for ingredients and nutrition are consistent with the new photos we took.

Navigate to the nutrition table and compare values with the packaging you still have it. Otherwise, you can tap on the nutrition photo to zoom it.

Navigate to the “Ingredients” tab to compare the ingredients to what is on the packaging. Similarly, you can tap on the nutrition photo to zoom it.

6. Opening the edit mode

If you need to make adjustments for values, you can tap on “Edit” on the “Summary” tab.

7. Updating ingredients by performing a new text extraction

Navigate to the “Ingredients” section and long press on the current ingredients to select part of them.

Then using the Pop-Up menu that just appeared, tap on the 3 little dots, and then tap on “Select all”.

Alternatively, you can use the 2 circular cursors that appeared to select all the ingredients, but by experience, that is more complex to do.

You should see the “Extract ingredients” button appear. Tap on it to perform the automatic text extraction.

When the text extraction is complete, you should see the extracted text appear. There might be some extra information before and after the ingredients, that we need to remove that before validating the list of ingredients.

Long press and select the extra text before using the cursors and use “Cut” to remove it.

Long press and select the extra text using the cursors after and use “Cut” to remove it.

8. Checking the ingredients carefully before validating them

Then check carefully but each ingredient matches what's on the packaging or on the photo. Again you can tap the photo to zoom it.

  • Take extra care about the little stars that may indicate specific things about the ingredients. Ensure that the OCR hasn't converted it to an apostrophe or something else.
  • Ensure that commas have been properly transcribed.
  • Ensure that there is no missing ingredient or typo.

Once you are satisfied, you can tap on “This looks Good”. Tapping on “Too bad, ignore the ingredients” will delete everything.

Once you have the ingredients, you should see a little green check next to it.

Be careful: at this point, the ingredients haven't been saved !

9. Saving immediately your work, or moving on to editing nutrition

To save them you have two options.

You can tap on the back arrow on the top right of your screen and confirm.

You can scroll to the bottom of your screen and tap on “Next”. This will take you to the Nutrition tab. The “Save” button is at the bottom of the Nutrition tab.

The top arrow makes sense if you want to just edit the ingredients.

Going to the nutrition tab makes sense if you need to adjust values there as well.

10. Updating nutrition

You can adjust the nutrition values as needed. Please be careful about units and whether the product information is “per proportion” or “per 100g/mL”.

The portion size is just below the choice between “per 100g/100mL” and “per portion”.

At the very top, you also have the option to indicate that the product doesn't have any nutrition information, if that is the case.

11. Saving your edits

Once you are happy, you can tap on the green button called “Save edits”. Tapping the back arrow and validating your changes will have the same result.

After your update is successful, you should see a green confirmation and updated scores and analysis if relevant.

12. Well done !

Congratulations, you've done your first product update on Open Food Facts using mobile !
